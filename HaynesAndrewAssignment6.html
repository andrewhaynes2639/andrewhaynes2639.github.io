Why use a to do list?

A to do list can be a huge help for those who cannot remember things they plan to do in the near future. I am one of these people who forgets something a couple minutes after thinking it if I do not write it down. A well-written to do list will prioritize what needs to be done first and shows when items have been completed. This can improve productivity drastically.

How to use a to do list in the workplace.

In a workplace, a to do list can improve productivity for everyone in the workplace by allowing easy viewability of any item that needs to be completed. For lower-ranking members, they can login to the webpage and see what needs to be completed, and actually complete those items. Once they have completed them they can mark them as complete. From there a commander or some type of leadership could view what has been completed and track deadlines when certain items are coming due.

Managing your to do list and making it more efficient.

A to do list can be a confusing and complicated process to make (like it has been for me), but will really pay off in the long run. The more you put into it, the more you will get out of it. For instance, if the developer continues to improve the list by incorporating an x to the left or right of the item. This would allow the users to delete items that are no longer needed. Or maybe an organization requires certain criteria that could be customized to better support the unit or workplace. These small but effective changes can make it easier for users in your organization.
